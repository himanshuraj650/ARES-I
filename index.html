JEZERO CRATER - SOL 1

A New World
Begins Here.

The hatch opens. The first boot presses into rust-red soil. Gravity at 38% pulls gently. The sky is butterscotch at dawn, blue near the sun. Every breath is a miracle. Every step is a message to every human who ever looked up and dreamed.

SOL 3

Perchlorate Detected

Soil chemistry analysis reveals perchlorate salts - the same compound that may enable microbial life in extreme environments on Earth.

The rover drills 2m into regolith and extracts core samples for the onboard laboratory. Spectrometer results suggest subsurface brine.

GAS
SOL 12

Methane Spike

Atmospheric sensors detect a 21 ppb methane spike - 8x background levels. Source unknown. Biological origin cannot be ruled out.

Methane on Mars is unstable - destroyed by UV within centuries. A fresh spike implies a recent source: volcanic, geological, or biological.
READ MORE
ICE
SOL 28

Subsurface Ice

Ground-penetrating radar confirms a 15m layer of water ice at 20m depth beneath Jezero Crater - enough water to fill hundreds of Olympic pools.

This changes everything. Water ice at accessible depth means fuel can be synthesised in situ. A permanent base just became viable.
READ MORE

Microscopic structures in ancient lakebed sediment bear striking morphological resemblance to fossilised stromatolites on Earth. Analysis ongoing.

Confirmation would be the greatest discovery in human history. The scientific community holds its breath. Repeat testing is underway.
